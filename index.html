Assassin's Creed

Assassin’s Creed (2007) was developed by Ubisoft and set during the Third Crusade (1191 AD), bringing players into the world of stealth, parkour, and historical intrigue. Step into the shoes of Altaïr Ibn-La'Ahad, a master assassin on a mission to eliminate key Templar targets and restore balance to the Holy Land. Through the Animus, modern-day Desmond Miles relives his ancestor’s past, uncovering the hidden war between Assassins and Templars. With immersive open-world exploration and dynamic gameplay, the legend begins here. Are you ready to embrace the Creed?

Assassin's Creed II

Assassin’s Creed II (2009) was released by Ubisoft, elevating the franchise to legendary status with its rich storytelling and breathtaking world. Set in the heart of the Italian Renaissance, it follows Ezio Auditore da Firenze, a young nobleman whose world is shattered by betrayal. From a reckless youth to a master assassin, Ezio embarks on a path of vengeance, uncovering an ancient war between the Assassins and Templars. Explore the vibrant streets of Florence, Venice, and Rome, scale towering cathedrals, and forge alliances with icons like Leonardo da Vinci. With refined parkour, deadly combat, and a world teeming with secrets, Assassin’s Creed II isn’t just a game—it’s a masterpiece of history, adventure, and destiny.

Assassin's Creed Brotherhood

Assassin’s Creed: Brotherhood (2010) was released by Ubisoft, taking the franchise to new heights with its rich narrative, breathtaking world, and evolved gameplay. Set in the heart of Renaissance Rome, the game follows Ezio Auditore da Firenze, now a master Assassin, as he wages war against the tyrannical Borgia family. With the city under Templar control, Ezio must rebuild the Assassin Brotherhood, recruit and train new warriors, and strike from the shadows to restore freedom. Rome becomes his battlefield—its towering landmarks, bustling streets, and hidden passageways alive with danger and intrigue. Featuring refined combat, fluid parkour, strategic Brotherhood management, and the franchise’s first multiplayer mode, Assassin’s Creed: Brotherhood isn’t just a game—it’s a revolution.

Assassin's Creed Revelations

Assassin’s Creed: Revelations (2011) was released by Ubisoft, delivering a breathtaking finale to Ezio Auditore’s legendary journey. Now an aging Master Assassin, Ezio travels to Constantinople, the heart of the Ottoman Empire, in search of the lost Keys to Masyaf, unlocking the long-buried secrets of Altaïr Ibn-La'Ahad. As the Templars tighten their grip on the city, Ezio must embrace new tools like the Hookblade for faster parkour and master the art of bomb crafting to outmaneuver his enemies. Meanwhile, Desmond Miles, trapped in the depths of the Animus, fights to piece together his fractured mind. With an emotional narrative, stunning historical landscapes, and refined gameplay, Assassin’s Creed: Revelations is not just a game—it is the ultimate convergence of past, present, and destiny.

Assassin's Creed III

Assassin’s Creed III (2012) was released by Ubisoft, bringing the franchise to the heart of the American Revolution. Set in the late 18th century, the game follows Ratonhnhaké:ton, also known as Connor, a half-Mohawk, half-English Assassin fighting to protect his people and secure freedom for a new nation. From the bustling streets of Boston and New York to the vast, untamed Frontier, Connor uses his lethal combat skills, parkour, and stealth to hunt down the Templars who seek control over the revolution. With dynamic weather, naval warfare, and an evolving world shaped by war and politics, Assassin’s Creed III delivers a powerful, action-packed journey through one of history’s most defining moments.

Assassin's Creed IV Black Flag

Assassin’s Creed IV: Black Flag (2013) was released by Ubisoft, taking the franchise to the golden age of piracy. Set in the early 18th century Caribbean, the game follows Edward Kenway, a ruthless pirate-turned-Assassin who navigates the deadly waters between freedom and loyalty. From the bustling ports of Havana, Kingston, and Nassau to the open seas filled with naval battles, treasure hunts, and hidden secrets, Edward carves his own path in a world ruled by empires and outlaws. With breathtaking naval combat, seamless open-world exploration, and a story of betrayal, brotherhood, and the pursuit of power, Assassin’s Creed IV: Black Flag is the ultimate pirate adventure, where the creed meets the sea.

Assassin's Creed Rogue

Assassin's Creed Rogue (2014), forged by Ubisoft Sofia , stands as one of the franchise's most captivating and unconventional chapters. Set amidst the chaos of the Seven Years' War, the game unravels the tale of Shay Patrick Cormac, an Assassin turned Templar whose rebellion against his own brotherhood sends shockwaves through history. As icy winds sweep across the North Atlantic and forgotten secrets hide within the River Valley, players embark on a journey of betrayal, redemption, and self-discovery. With a chilling atmosphere, dynamic naval warfare, and a narrative that questions the very fabric of loyalty, Assassin's Creed Rogue casts players into the shadows — where the line between hero and villain fades into the cold silence of the past.

Assassin's Creed Unity

Assassin's Creed Unity (2014) was crafted by Ubisoft Montreal , painting a breathtaking portrait of revolutionary Paris during the tumultuous French Revolution. In the midst of crumbling monarchies and rising chaos, the story follows Arno Dorian, a young Assassin whose pursuit of redemption intertwines with a city on the brink of transformation. With its stunningly recreated Notre-Dame Cathedral, bustling crowds, and blood-stained cobblestone streets, Unity breathes life into Paris like never before. The game introduces fluid parkour mechanics, deep customization, and seamless co-op missions, allowing players to carve their own legend amidst the shadows of rebellion. As the revolution ignites and secrets unravel, Assassin's Creed Unity blends betrayal, vengeance, and love into a symphony of chaos — where every blade drawn reshapes the course of history.

Assassin's Creed Syndicate

Assassin's Creed Syndicate (2015) was masterfully crafted by Ubisoft Quebec , casting players into the smog-filled streets of Victorian London at the height of the Industrial Revolution. Amid the clash of steel and ambition, the game follows the charismatic twins Jacob and Evie Frye, leading a hidden rebellion against the iron grip of the Templar Order. With London's skyline pierced by towering factories and its underworld ruled by ruthless gangs, Syndicate blends fast-paced combat, stealth, and dynamic parkour into a symphony of chaos. The dual protagonist system offers two distinct journeys — Jacob's relentless pursuit of power and Evie's calculated quest for truth. In a world where corruption thrives in the shadows, Assassin's Creed Syndicate tells a tale of rebellion, unity, and the fight to free a city bound by chains of progress.

Assassin's Creed Origins

Assassin's Creed Origins was masterfully crafted by Ubisoft Montreal and released in 2017, transporting players to the mystical lands of Ancient Egypt — where gods walked among men and empires rose beneath the burning sun. Set during the reign of Cleopatra, the game follows Bayek of Siwa, a lone Medjay warrior bound by vengeance, whose journey unravels a web of betrayal that echoes through the sands of time. From the towering pyramids of Giza to the shimmering waters of the Nile, every corner of Egypt breathes with life, mystery, and forgotten legends. With its redefined RPG mechanics, fluid combat system, and a world teeming with secrets, Assassin's Creed Origins is not just the story of one man — it is the birth of a brotherhood destined to protect the shadows of history.

Assassin's Creed Odyssey

Assassin's Creed Odyssey was made by Ubisoft Quebec and released in 2018, immersing players in the sunlit beauty and war-torn landscapes of Ancient Greece during the Peloponnesian War. The game follows the journey of either Alexios or Kassandra, mercenaries with a hidden lineage tied to the legendary Spartan bloodline. Odyssey marks a bold evolution in the franchise, embracing deep RPG elements, branching storylines, and player-driven choices that shape the fate of Greece. From the towering peaks of Mount Olympus to the shimmering waters of the Aegean Sea, the world pulses with myth, danger, and ancient secrets. With its breathtaking open world and epic narrative of betrayal, destiny, and forgotten gods, Assassin's Creed Odyssey invites players to forge their own legacy in a land where heroes are born and legends are written in blood.

Assassin's Creed Valhalla

Assassin's Creed Valhalla was made by Ubisoft Montreal and released in 2020, plunging players into the harsh yet breathtaking world of Ninth-century England during the Viking Age. The game follows Eivor, a fierce Viking warrior driven by glory and survival, as they lead their clan from the icy shores of Norway to the fertile lands of England in search of a new home. With its sprawling open world, deep RPG mechanics, and brutal combat system, Valhalla blends Viking mythology with the hidden war between Assassins and Templars. From snow-covered fjords to war-torn kingdoms, every step is a journey of conquest, betrayal, and destiny. As alliances are forged and enemies fall, Assassin's Creed Valhalla tells a saga where every battle shapes the legacy of a warrior bound by both honor and fate.
